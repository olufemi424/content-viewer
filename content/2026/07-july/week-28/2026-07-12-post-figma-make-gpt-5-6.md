## Hook
If you use Figma to turn ideas into prototypes, this update matters today.

## Why this matters
Most AI design demos look good until you try to turn them into something usable. The problem is usually the first pass: weak layout fidelity, too much prompting, and extra cleanup before a prototype is worth sharing. On July 9, 2026, Figma added GPT-5.6 to Figma Make and said the new model improves both first-pass quality and speed. For creators, solo builders, and operators, the practical shift is simple: less time wrestling with the tool and more time shipping a prototype that is close enough to test.

## Mechanism
The mechanism is better design-to-build generation inside an existing workflow. Figma's launch post says GPT-5.6 improves first-pass output across clean UI, responsive layouts, and higher-fidelity interactions. The same post also says the model is stronger when building from an existing Figma design file, where layout, visual hierarchy, spacing, and proportions carry over more faithfully. Figma's release notes add two rollout details that matter for adoption: GPT-5.6 is available in the Figma Make model picker, and it is available on all plans. In plain English, Figma is trying to reduce the gap between rough concept, existing design, and working prototype.

## Proof/use case
The proof is that Figma published the update across multiple official surfaces on July 9, 2026, and the details line up. The blog post shows concrete examples: a stock-tracking app generated from one prompt, an audio player built from an existing design file, and a bookshelf e-commerce prototype that adapted across screen sizes. The release notes repeat that GPT-5.6 is now available in Figma Make on all plans, which supports the practical takeaway that this is a live workflow change, not just a teaser. The clearest use case for Olufemi's audience is a creator or solo builder starting with a rough mock, then using Make to generate a more usable prototype faster before recording a demo, testing a landing page idea, or handing a concept to a teammate.
