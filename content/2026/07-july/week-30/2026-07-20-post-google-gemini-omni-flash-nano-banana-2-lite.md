## Hook
Google just made a full image-to-video workflow feel like one stack instead of three separate tools.

## Why this matters
This matters because most creators and solo builders do not struggle to generate one cool asset. They struggle to turn ideas into repeatable content without burning time across separate image, video, and editing tools. Google's June 30 rollout pairs Nano Banana 2 Lite for fast, low-cost image generation with Gemini Omni Flash for conversational video generation and editing inside the same Google AI Studio and Gemini API workflow. For a creator or operator audience, the practical shift is simple: the new bottleneck is less about "can AI make media?" and more about whether one stack can move from draft image to editable video fast enough to fit a real publishing loop.

## Mechanism
According to Google's official launch posts and Gemini API release notes, Nano Banana 2 Lite is generally available as `gemini-3.1-flash-lite-image`, while Gemini Omni Flash is in public preview as `gemini-omni-flash-preview`. Google says Nano Banana 2 Lite delivers text-to-image outputs in about 4 seconds and costs $0.034 per 1K image, making it the speed-first image layer. Google also says Omni Flash can generate 3 to 10 second 720p videos, animate still images, and then let you refine those clips conversationally through the Interactions API. The workflow implication is that one prompt chain can go from rough visual concept to short editable video without exporting between multiple vendors.

## Proof/use case
The strongest proof is that Google is not pitching these as isolated demos. In its Cloud launch post, Google explicitly frames the pairing around high-volume ideation, A/B testing, and creative agentic workflows, and says Omni Flash is priced at $0.10 per second of video output. Adobe is also quoted in that same post saying the new models help creators move faster from idea to finished content inside Firefly. A realistic use case for Olufemi's audience is simple: generate several thumbnail-style scene concepts with Nano Banana 2 Lite, choose the strongest one, animate it into a 3 to 10 second social clip with Omni Flash, then use conversational edits to swap text, relight the scene, or tweak the product shot without restarting the workflow.
