## Hook
AI agents do not just need better prompts anymore. They need their own accounts, limits, and permission rails.

## Why this matters
This matters for creators, solo builders, and operators because the next wave of agent products is not just about answering questions. It is about completing paid actions safely. If your agent can research but cannot pay for data, place a trade, or execute a budgeted workflow without exposing your whole account, it hits a wall fast. Coinbase is useful here because it treats the agent like a bounded economic actor instead of just another API client.

## Mechanism
Coinbase announced Coinbase for Agents on June 11, 2026 as a CLI and MCP workflow that connects an AI agent to a Coinbase account under user-defined limits. The official launch frames it around isolated agent access for trades, payments, and workflow execution rather than full unrestricted account control. Coinbase Developer documentation also positions it alongside agentic wallet tooling, which makes the product story clearer: agents are getting account-like permissions, not just tool-call wrappers.

## Proof/use case
The proof is the launch itself plus how Coinbase described immediate use cases. According to Coinbase, an agent can trade, pay, and run workflows on a user's behalf within controlled limits. Supporting coverage from CoinDesk and TechCrunch both focused on the same shift: agent accounts that can transact, plus a path toward paying for tools or premium research through x402-style flows. The practical use case for builders is obvious: give an agent a narrow wallet and task scope instead of handing it a raw key and hoping for the best.
