## Hook
Notion just made a bigger AI move than most people realize: Claude can now run as an agent inside your workspace instead of living in a separate chat tab.

## Why this matters
This matters because the real bottleneck for most creator and operator workflows is not model quality anymore. It is context switching. Your ideas live in docs, your tasks live in boards, your approvals live in comments, and your AI work often lives somewhere else entirely. Notion's Claude agent beta is a direct attempt to collapse that gap. If your workspace is already where planning and execution happen, putting the agent there changes the operating model from "ask AI, then copy the result back" to "assign work where the work already lives."

## Mechanism
Notion's June 24 help doc says Claude agents in Notion are in beta for Business and Enterprise plans, are hosted by Notion via Anthropic's infrastructure, and run on Notion credits per run. The setup flow is native to Notion: go to `Agents`, create a new Claude agent, choose a template or start from scratch, then configure instructions, triggers, and connections. The important control layer is permissions. Notion says each Claude agent can only access what is explicitly shared with it, and those permissions are set per agent rather than inherited from the person who starts the run. Notion also says Claude agents can chat in the workspace, work from shared docs and task boards, and create or update content if they have edit access. They cannot browse the web, and they cannot call other agents during a session.

## Proof/use case
The strongest proof is that this rollout lines up with Notion's bigger Developer Platform push from May. In that release and companion blog post, Notion said the goal was to make external agents first-class workspace participants so teams could chat with them, assign work, and track progress in one place. Claude is now the clearest live example of that strategy. For a creator or solo operator, the immediate use case is simple: keep your content brief, task board, and draft in Notion, then assign the Claude agent to turn the brief into a first pass or update a page with the next version. That is not the same as opening Claude in another app and pasting the result back manually. The workflow value is shared visibility, permissions, and execution inside the same workspace where the team already reviews work.
