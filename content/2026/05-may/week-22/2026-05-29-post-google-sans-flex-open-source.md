## Hook
Google just gave builders a serious UI upgrade for free.

## Why this matters
If you ship products solo, typography is usually where polish breaks. Open-sourcing Google Sans Flex means you can use one variable font across product UI, marketing pages, and design systems without paying for a premium family or juggling multiple font files.

## Mechanism
Google Sans Flex is a variable font, so one family can cover multiple visual styles through axes like weight and width. Practically, that means fewer font files, tighter visual consistency, and faster iteration when you need to tune readability at different breakpoints.

## Proof/use case
Google Fonts now lists Google Sans Flex directly, including variable instances and web-ready loading. A solo builder can define a small typography token set once, then map the same family across app shell, docs, and landing pages with different weight/width settings instead of switching typefaces per surface.
